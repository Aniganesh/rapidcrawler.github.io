Introduction

Probability is the study of randomness and uncertainity. Probability theory is the foundation on which descriptive and predictive analytics models are built. Probability helps in providing ways of gaining new insights into the behavior of many phenomena.

Statistics provides methods for organizing and summarizing data, for drawing conclusions based on information conatined in Data.

Data results from making observation on either a single variable, i.e. Univariate Data, or observations on multiple variables, i.e. Multivariate Data.
The investigator may simply waish to describe and summarize important aspects of the colleceted data, i.e. Descriptive Statistics. This could be either graphical in nature, or numerical in nature.

In absence of Population, i.e. collection of data points of interest, the investigator may have to work on Sample data, i.e. a subset or a portion of Population. In such situations, the investigator will have to use sample information to draw conclusions/inferences about the Population data set. This form of generalizing Population from Sample is called as Inferential Statistics.

Collection of data can be represented in numerical form, i.e. a set of observation. Or in a graphical form, i.e. Dot-Chart

Frequency can also be graphically presented in the form of Histogram

Or, a smoothed histogram called as Density Estimate

A histogram, or density estimate could either be Symmetric (left half being mirror of right half), or it could be Skewed.


Sample Space & Events

As stated above, Probability is the study of randomness and uncertainity. And Probability helps in providing ways of gaining new insights into the behavior of many phenomena.

And any activity or process whose outcome is subject to uncertainity or randomness is called as Experiment. E.g. tossing of a coin, selecting a card from a deck, weiging of a loaf of bread, measuring strength of metal beams, etc.

And the set of all possible outcomes from an experiment is called as Sample Space.
